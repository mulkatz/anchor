## Project Identity

**Project Name:** Anxiety Buddy
**Internal Codename:** Project Anchor
**Stage:** Polishing Phase (transitioning from Greenfield Development)
**Target Audience:** Gen Z (18-26 years old)
**Core Purpose:** Immediate Relief Tool for Anxiety & Panic Attacks

---

## The Vision

This is **NOT** a wellness app with gamification. This is a **therapeutic interface**.

### Core Principles

- **The Interface IS the Therapy** - Every pixel, every animation, every haptic pulse is designed to ground and de-escalate panic.
- **Evidence-Based** - Built on Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) + Somatic Grounding techniques.
- **Mobile-First** - Native iOS/Android with Capacitor. The app must feel native, not web-wrapped.

Imagine the deep ocean at night. Viscous, slow-moving, calming darkness punctuated by gentle glowing life forms.

### Mood Board Keywords

- **Deep ocean darkness** - Safety in the void
- **Bioluminescent jellyfish** - Soft, pulsing light
- **Viscous liquid motion** - Slow, deliberate
- **Frosted glass surfaces** - Depth, layering
- **Warm glows in cold darkness** - Hope, guidance

## Clinical Foundation

### Therapeutic Approaches

1. **Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)** - Restructuring anxious thought patterns
2. **Somatic Grounding** - Body-based techniques to interrupt panic
3. **5-4-3-2-1 Technique** - Sensory grounding through sight, touch, sound
4. **4-7-8 Breathing** - Parasympathetic nervous system activation (Dr. Andrew Weil)
5. **Somatic Experiencing** - Trauma-informed body awareness (Peter Levine)

### Why This Matters

During a panic attack, the prefrontal cortex (rational brain) is offline. The user cannot:

- Read complex instructions
- Make decisions
- Focus on gamification

**Solution:** Multi-sensory, body-based interventions that bypass cognitive overload.

## Target Audience Deep Dive

### Gen Z (18-26 years old)

**Characteristics:**

- Digital natives - expect native app quality
- Anxiety epidemic - 1 in 3 experience panic attacks
- Skeptical of "wellness culture" - reject toxic positivity
- Value authenticity - want real help, not platitudes
- Mobile-dependent - phone is primary device

**Design Implications:**

- Dark aesthetic (rejects bright "happy" wellness apps)
- Viscous animations (calming, not energizing)
- Minimal text (cognitive overload during panic)
- Haptics as therapy (tangible, grounding)
- No gamification (anxiety isn't a game)

---

## What This Is NOT

❌ **NOT a meditation app** - No 30-day challenges, no streak tracking
❌ **NOT a mood tracker** - No emoji journals, no data visualization for sake of data
❌ **NOT a social app** - No community features, no sharing
❌ **NOT a replacement for therapy** - Emergency relief tool, not long-term treatment
❌ **NOT wellness culture** - No toxic positivity, no "good vibes only"

---

## What This IS

✅ **An emergency tool** - For immediate relief during panic attacks
✅ **A therapeutic interface** - Every interaction is clinically informed
✅ **A grounding experience** - Multi-sensory de-escalation
✅ **A safe space** - Dark, calm, non-judgmental
✅ **A bridge to help** - Prominent crisis resources, encourages professional care

---

## Success Criteria

**Primary Goal:** Reduce acute anxiety symptoms during panic attacks

**Measured By:**

- SOS flow completion rate (target: >80%)
- Time to completion (benchmark: 2-3 minutes)
- Self-reported anxiety reduction (1-5 scale)
- Repeat usage (indicates effectiveness)

**NOT Measured By:**

- Daily active users (anxiety isn't daily)
- Session duration (longer ≠ better)
- Feature engagement (less is more)
